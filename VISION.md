# The YawningFace Vision

*Everybody scrolls.* The kid on the night train watching TikToks until sunrise.
The retired father deep in Facebook. The businessman scrolling LinkedIn. All
ages, all classes - because trillion-dollar companies pay the best engineers
alive to maximize the time you spend inside a vertical feed. That's not a
conspiracy theory; it's their business model, stated plainly in every earnings
call.

Willpower loses that fight. Nothing in a normal day competes with the limbic
resonance of an infinite feed. So we do what people have always done with
substances that beat their willpower: **we don't keep it in the house.**
YawningFace blocks the substance - on every device you own, at the moments
you chose in advance.

The battle against digital slavery can be won. This repo is the weapon:
**free, open source, cross-device, and yours.**

## The one insight everything else follows from

Every blocker dies the same death. One legitimate moment where the block is
wrong - you're at the gym and need Instagram for ten seconds to add a friend,
you're on vacation and the "workday mornings" wall is absurd at a holiday
breakfast - and the user doesn't tweak the config. They uninstall. And they
never install again. **One bad moment costs the whole war.**

Existing blockers are built like fortresses and die like fortresses. Ours is
built like a living thing: it bends early so it never has to break.

## Principles

1. **Sustainable beats radical.** The right configuration is the one you'll
   still be living with in six months, not the strictest one you can bear
   today. A block that fights your real life is a block you will delete.
2. **Smart friction, not walls.** You can always get out in a legitimate
   moment - but the friction grows if you abuse it: more steps, more waiting,
   more questions. Never a virus you can't remove; never a door with no lock.
3. **The config is alive, and the coach rewrites it.** Real lives need rules
   too rich to hand-configure: radical mornings, softer when you leave home,
   work sessions with written-reason exceptions, moral-limit sites that never
   unblock. So the configuration is written - and *rewritten* - in
   conversation: "I'm on vacation next week, relax the mornings." Every AI
   blocker on the market is a doorman arguing about one unlock. Ours is a
   tailor who refits the rules as your life changes. **And the AI is opt-in,
   always**: the config is a JSON file you own and can edit with any text
   editor, forever.
4. **One brain, every device.** Blocking Twitter on your phone means nothing
   if it's open on your laptop. One config document; iPhone, Mac, Windows,
   and browser all enforce it locally. Start a working session and it lands
   everywhere at once - no account required for the basics, offline-first
   always.
5. **The physical world is a feature.** A cheap NFC sticker on the kitchen
   shelf can be the key that unlocks your phone - same mechanic the $60
   pucks sell, for the price of a sticker, with no server dependency and no
   subscription. Locations too: the gym relaxes the rules, the library
   tightens them.
6. **Friends keep you honest.** Screen time shared with people you chose
   beats streaks and badges. Accountability is social, not gamified.
7. **Agent-native.** The blocker speaks CLI. "How did I do last week?" is a
   command line away - for you, and for the agents working on your behalf.
8. **Taste is 99% of this.** Install flows that feel like Apple, copy that
   respects the reader, dark navy and one yellow accent. No AI slop, no
   guilt-tripping, no dark patterns - we don't fight manipulation with
   manipulation.
9. **Free. Open source. Forever.** The incumbents charge $40–100/year for
   closed fortresses. This is MIT-licensed and self-hostable. People stop
   being the product here.

## Non-goals

This monorepo is a **blocker**, not a productivity suite. No pomodoro timers,
no lofi players, no note widgets - that sprawl killed the 2025 attempt in
four days flat (the git history has the receipts). Shipped side-products live
in their own repos. If a feature doesn't reduce unwanted screen time, it
doesn't merge.

## Lineage

Third generation of one stubborn idea: **Portorium** (2024) - a Roman border
where you stop and pay before entering; **Block / YawningBlock** (2025)  - 
the cross-platform vision, stopped by a then-unsolvable iPhone; and
**YawningFace** (2026) - after the January 2026 breakthrough made persistent
iPhone blocking real, with coding agents to hold the breadth, and an LLM
finally good enough to be the tailor.

The name is the point: healthy technology should make you yawn. 😴 is the
resting state. 😎 is what blocking feels like.
